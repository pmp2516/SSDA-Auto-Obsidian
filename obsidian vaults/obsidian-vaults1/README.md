# Obsidian Vault Pack — Three Organization Methods

Three fully-populated Obsidian vaults, each demonstrating a different note-taking philosophy. All vaults share a fictional cast of characters (Elena Vasquez, Tom Reyes, Sarah Kim), a running project (Meridian v2.0 launch), and common reference material — so you can see how the same information is organized differently across methods.

---

## Vault 1 — PARA Method (`vault-1-PARA`)

**Philosophy**: Organize by actionability, not topic.
- **Projects** — things with a deadline and definition of done
- **Areas** — ongoing responsibilities (no end date)
- **Resources** — reference material for future use
- **Archive** — inactive material, kept for reference

**Best for**: People who want their notes to serve their *work* directly. Every note lives somewhere based on how relevant it is to your current life.

**Open with**: `Projects/Q3-Product-Launch.md` → follow the wiki-links to see how Projects, Areas, Resources, and People interconnect.

### File count: 18 notes + 5 templates

---

## Vault 2 — Zettelkasten (`vault-2-Zettelkasten`)

**Philosophy**: Atomic, linked ideas. Build a second brain of permanent knowledge.

- **00-Inbox** — raw fleeting thoughts, unprocessed
- **10-Literature** — notes from books, papers, talks (in your own words)
- **20-Permanent** — single-idea atomic notes (the "Zettels")
- **30-Structure** — Maps of Content (MOC): index notes that connect permanent notes by theme

**Best for**: Researchers, writers, anyone who wants their notes to *generate new ideas* through unexpected connections.

**Open with**: `10-Literature/lit-2026-041.md` → follow links through permanent notes to the MOC.

### File count: 14 notes + 3 templates

---

## Vault 3 — Topic Hub (`vault-3-TopicHub`)

**Philosophy**: Organize by subject. Hub notes (MOCs) are the entry points; all other notes live flat in topic folders.

- **Hubs** — Map of Content notes, one per major topic area
- **Notes/[Topic]** — subject notes organised by folder
- **People** — person notes with relationship context
- **Daily** — daily notes as a log and connection record

**Best for**: People with distinct interest areas who want a clear navigable map without strict processing workflows.

**Open with**: `Hubs/Technology-Hub.md` → explore from there.

### File count: 16 notes + 4 templates

## How to Open

1. Download and unzip
2. Open Obsidian → "Open folder as vault"
3. Select the vault folder you want to open
4. Enable "Readable line length" in Settings → Editor for best reading experience
5. Open the Graph View (Ctrl/Cmd+G) to see the link network
