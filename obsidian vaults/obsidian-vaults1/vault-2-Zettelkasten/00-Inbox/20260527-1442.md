# Fleeting note — 2026-05-27 14:42

Interesting thought during lunch: the reason I procrastinate on complex writing tasks might be less about laziness and more about fear of discovering that my ideas are less coherent than they feel in my head.

Writing forces specificity. A vague feeling of "I understand this" can survive indefinitely, but the moment you try to write a sentence, the gaps become visible.

This is related to something I read in Kahneman — the illusion of understanding (see lit notes). But it's also about the emotional cost of realising your mental model is wrong.
