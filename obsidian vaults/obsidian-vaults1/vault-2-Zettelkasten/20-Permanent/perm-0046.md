# perm-0046 — The K-word experiment as a pedagogical tool

**Core idea**: The best way to teach the availability heuristic is through the K-word experiment (Tversky & Kahneman, 1973), because the bias is detectable in the learner's own reasoning in real time.

**The experiment**: Ask someone whether more common English words begin with the letter K or have K as the third letter. Most people say "begins with K." In fact, approximately 3× more common words have K as the third letter (king, like, make, take…). Words starting with K (key, kind, keep…) are easier to retrieve because we index words by their first letter.

**Why this is pedagogically powerful**:
- The learner is demonstrably wrong in their own judgment
- The mechanism is immediately visible: "I couldn't think of words with K in 3rd position"
- No statistics are needed — the demonstration is the explanation
- No emotional stakes → no motivated reasoning → the bias is accepted

**Contrast with high-stakes examples** (plane crashes, crime rates): In those cases, the learner may resist the correction because of motivated reasoning or prior beliefs. The K-word experiment strips away confounding variables.

## Application

Use the K-word experiment *before* introducing the concept in teaching contexts. Get the audience to make the prediction, reveal the answer, then explain the mechanism. This is far more memorable than explaining the mechanism first.
