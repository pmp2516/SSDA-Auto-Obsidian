# Fleeting note — 2026-05-25 18:30

Listening to a talk on expert intuition. The speaker made a point that stuck: *expertise is pattern recognition, not rule application*. Grandmasters don't "calculate" — they see the position.

But there's a dark side: patterns can become prisons. The expert stops seeing what doesn't fit the pattern. "When you have a hammer, everything looks like a nail" is really "when you have a well-trained hammer-pattern, you literally fail to perceive screws."

This feels connected to the availability heuristic (→ [[../20-Permanent/perm-0045]]) but it's subtly different. Availability is about frequency estimation. This is about *perceptual narrowing* — the thing you don't see can't trigger any heuristic at all.
