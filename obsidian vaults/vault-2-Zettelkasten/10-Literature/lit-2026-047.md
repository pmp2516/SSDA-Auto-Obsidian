# lit-2026-047 — Digital Minimalism (Newport)

## Source

Cal Newport, "Digital Minimalism" (2019). Follow-up to [[lit-2026-044]] (Deep Work).

## Summary

Newport argues that the relationship most people have with their smartphones and social media apps is not one they chose — it was engineered. The intermittent reinforcement schedules built into these platforms exploit the same psychological mechanisms as slot machines. The solution is not moderation but a deliberate philosophical commitment to using technology only when it provides substantial value relative to its costs.

**The Digital Minimalism philosophy**: "Use technology to support your values, not to fragment your attention."

**The 30-day digital declutter**: Complete abstinence from optional technologies for 30 days. Then reintroduce only those that pass a high bar: they must actively serve something you deeply value, and must be the *best* way to serve that value.

**Solitude deprivation**: A key concept — Newport argues modern smartphone use has eliminated all solitude from life. The mind needs time alone to process experience, form identity, and generate insight. Constant input prevents this.

## My Reading

The "solitude deprivation" concept landed harder than the rest of the book. I've noticed that my best ideas come on walks without my phone, in the shower, or in the gap between waking and reaching for the device. Those gaps are disappearing.

Applied change: phone in bedroom until noon (already doing this from [[lit-2026-044]] protocol, but for different reasons — the solitude angle is a stronger motivator for me personally).
